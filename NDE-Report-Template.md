**Writing Prompts:**
- How would you define a Near-Death Experience (NDE) for someone unfamiliar with the term?

- Why is studying NDEs important for science, healthcare, or society?

## Literature Review (500-650 words)

*Organize by themes related to your research question:*

### Theme 1: Personality Changes After NDE

**Writing Prompts:**
- What does existing research say about personality shifts following NDEs?
- What specific personality traits change (e.g., empathy, emotional sensitivity)?
- Which researchers/studies documented these changes?
- Are findings consistent across studies?

### Theme 2: Values Transformation Post-NDE

**Writing Prompts:**
- How do NDErs' life values change after their experience?
- What shifts occur from materialism to spirituality/altruism?
- What evidence supports these value changes?
- Are these changes permanent or temporary?

### Theme 3: Changes in Religious/Spiritual Beliefs

**Writing Prompts:**
- How do NDEs influence belief in God, afterlife, or organized religion?
- Do people change their religious affiliation after NDEs?
- What percentage of NDErs report belief changes?
- How do cultural backgrounds influence belief changes?

### Theme 4: Shifts in Death Anxiety or Fear of Death

**Writing Prompts:**
- What does research show about death anxiety before and after NDEs?
- How dramatically do NDErs' fear of death change?
- What mechanism explains reduced death anxiety?
- Are there any cases where death anxiety increases?

### Knowledge Gaps

**Writing Prompts:**
- What is NOT yet studied about NDEs?
- Which populations are underrepresented in NDE research?
- What methodological approaches are missing?
- How does your research fill these gaps?

*Include APA-cited references for all literature discussed.*

## Objectives of the Research (100-150 words)

### Research Objectives

**Writing Prompts:**
- What are the main goals of your study? (state as 3-4 clear objectives)
- What specific outcomes do you want to measure?
- How do these align with your research question?

### Research Questions OR Hypotheses

**Writing Prompts for Research Questions:**
- RQ1: Do individuals who experience NDEs show greater personality changes compared to those without NDEs?
- RQ2: How do NDEs influence individuals' values and life priorities?
- RQ3: What changes occur in religious and spiritual beliefs after an NDE?
- RQ4: To what extent does experiencing an NDE reduce fear of death?

**Writing Prompts for Hypotheses (if using this format):**
- H1: Individuals with NDEs will report significantly higher empathy than pre-NDE levels.
- H2: NDE depth will be positively correlated with magnitude of belief changes.
- H3: NDErs will show lower death anxiety compared to control groups.

## Research Methodology (500-600 words)

### a. Research Design

**Writing Prompts:**
- What approach did your team choose? (mixed methods, survey, interviews, experimental, etc.)
- Why is this design appropriate for your research question?
- What are the strengths and limitations of this approach?
- Will you use both quantitative and qualitative methods?

### b. Sampling

**Writing Prompts:**
- Who is your target population? (age, location, characteristics)
- How did you identify and recruit participants?
- What sampling technique did you use? (snowball, matched, quota, random, etc.)
- How many participants did you recruit for each group?
- What were your inclusion/exclusion criteria?
- How did you achieve sample diversity?

### c. Data Collection

**Writing Prompts:**
- What are your primary data collection methods?
- Describe the questionnaire: How many questions? What topics? Format?
- What specific scales or instruments did you use? (provide names and brief descriptions)
- Did you conduct interviews? How long? How structured?
- How was the questionnaire pilot-tested and refined?
- Was data collected online, in-person, or mixed?

### d. Data Analysis

**Writing Prompts:**
- What statistical tests did you plan to use and why?
  - Descriptive statistics (means, frequencies, percentages)?
  - Inferential tests (t-tests, ANOVA, chi-square, correlations)?
  - Regression analysis?
- What qualitative analysis approach did you use? (thematic analysis, coding, etc.)
- What software did you use for analysis? (SPSS, Excel, NVivo, etc.)
- How did you ensure data quality and validity?

## Research Findings (600-700 words)

*Present results organized by research objectives, using tables and figures where appropriate. (All visuals must be generated from original data)*

### Participant Demographics

**Writing Prompts:**
- How many participants completed the study?
- What were their ages, genders, education levels?
- How long ago did they experience their NDE?
- What were their religious backgrounds?
- Create a table summarizing these characteristics

### Research Objective 1: Personality Changes

**Writing Prompts:**
- What personality measures did you use?
- What were the mean scores on these measures?
- How did NDE group compare to control groups statistically?
- Provide specific statistics: "Mean = X, SD = Y, t-test p-value = Z"
- Were changes significant?
- Create a bar chart or table showing comparisons
- Which personality traits showed the most change?

### Research Objective 2: Values Changes

**Writing Prompts:**
- How did participants rank their life values before vs. after?
- What percentage showed shifts from materialism to spirituality?
- Did altruistic behaviors increase?
- Provide descriptive statistics
- Include before/after comparison chart or table
- What were the most significant value shifts?

### Research Objective 3: Religious/Spiritual Belief Changes

**Writing Prompts:**
- What percentage believed in God before vs. after?
- How many experienced changes in religious affiliation?
- Did belief in afterlife increase?
- What percentage increased spiritual practice frequency?
- Create pie charts showing before/after beliefs
- What chi-square or statistical test results support these changes?

### Research Objective 4: Death Anxiety Changes

**Writing Prompts:**
- What death anxiety scores did participants report before vs. after?
- Was the change statistically significant?
- How did NDE group compare to control groups?
- What percentage reported no fear of death?
- Provide mean scores, standard deviations, test statistics
- Create line chart showing before/after trends

### Correlational Findings (if applicable)

**Writing Prompts:**
- Was NDE depth correlated with magnitude of changes?
- Did time since NDE affect results?
- Were there demographic factors that influenced outcomes?
- Provide correlation coefficients (r-values) and p-values

### Qualitative Findings (if applicable)

**Writing Prompts:**
- What major themes emerged from interviews?
- How many participants mentioned each theme?
- Provide 2-3 representative quotes that illustrate key themes
- How do qualitative findings support or enrich quantitative results?

## Conclusion, Recommendations, and Research Limitations (250-350 words)

### Conclusion

**Writing Prompts:**
- Restate your research objectives
- Provide a concise summary of major findings
- Did your findings support or refute your hypotheses/research questions?
- What is the overall answer to your research question: "Does NDE change personality, values, religious beliefs, or death anxiety?"
- What is the significance of this conclusion?

### Recommendations

**Writing Prompts for Practice:**
- What should healthcare providers know about NDEs?
- How could this inform end-of-life care protocols?
- Should support groups be established for NDErs?
- What training should medical professionals receive?

**Writing Prompts for Policy:**
- Should NDE education be integrated into medical curricula?
- What policies should hospitals implement regarding NDErs?
- How could public awareness of NDEs be improved?

**Writing Prompts for Future Research:**
- What questions remain unanswered?
- What larger or longer-term studies are needed?
- What populations should future research focus on?
- What methodological improvements could be made?
- Should cross-cultural comparative studies be conducted?

### Research Limitations

**Writing Prompts:**
- How large was your sample? Does size limit generalizability?
- Were there sampling biases? (e.g., snowball sampling of volunteers)
- Did retrospective reporting of "before" states introduce memory bias?
- Was your control group well-matched?
- Were there cultural or geographic limitations?
- Did time since NDE vary significantly across participants?
- What self-report or social desirability biases might exist?
- Could the cross-sectional design establish causality?
- Were there any data collection or analysis limitations?
- How do these limitations affect interpretation of findings?

## Appendices

### Appendix A: Participant Information Sheet / Recruitment Flyer

**Template:**
- What is this study about?
- Who can participate?
- What will participation involve? (time commitment, procedures)
- What are potential risks/benefits?
- How is privacy protected?
- Contact information for questions

### Appendix B: Informed Consent Form

**Template:**
- Study purpose and procedures
- Time commitment
- Voluntary participation / right to withdraw
- Confidentiality assurances
- Data storage and use
- Researcher contact information
- Ethics committee contact (if applicable)
- Participant signature and date

### Appendix C: Questionnaire

**Template:**
- Section 1: Screening and Demographics
  - Questions to verify NDE or control group status
  - Age, gender, education, occupation, religion
- Section 2: NDE Characteristics (if applicable)
  - Questions about NDE features (light, tunnel, beings, etc.)
- Section 3: Personality Measures
  - Sample Likert-scale questions about empathy, emotional sensitivity
- Section 4: Values and Life Priorities
  - Questions about materialism, altruism, life purpose
- Section 5: Religious/Spiritual Beliefs
  - Before/after questions about belief in God, afterlife, organized religion
- Section 6: Death Anxiety
  - Likert-scale questions about fear of death, acceptance of death

### Appendix D: Interview Guide (if applicable)

**Template:**
- Opening question to build rapport
- Grand tour question: "Can you tell me about your near-death experience?"
- Follow-up probes:
  - "How would you describe yourself before the experience?"
  - "What changed most significantly after your experience?"
  - "How do you think about death now compared to before?"
- Closing: "Is there anything else you'd like to share?"

### Appendix E: SPSS Output / Statistical Results

**Include:**
- Descriptive statistics tables
- t-test or ANOVA results
- Correlation matrices
- Chi-square results
- Any other statistical output relevant to your analysis

### Appendix F: Coding Scheme (if using qualitative analysis)

**Template:**
- List of themes identified
- Definition of each theme
- Example coded quotes
- Frequency of each theme

### Appendix G: Sample Interview Transcript Excerpts

- Include 1-2 short excerpts from interviews
- Show how transcripts were coded or analyzed

## Writing Guidelines

- **Word Count**: Aim for ~2,500 words (excluding References and Appendices)
- **Academic Tone**: Formal, objective, evidence-based
- **Clarity**: Minimize jargon; define technical terms on first use
- **Citations**: Use APA 7th edition in-text citations throughout
- **Figures and Tables**: Label clearly, provide titles and captions
- **Originality**: Ensure all content is your team's original work
- **No AI Content**: Do not include AI-generated text, tables, or figures in final report
