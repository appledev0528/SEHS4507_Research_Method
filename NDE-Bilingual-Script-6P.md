### 什么是濒死体验？(Slide: What is an NDE?) (2.25分钟 | 2 min 15 sec)

**中文 Chinese:**
"濒死体验，简称NDE，是指个体在面临死亡或生命危险时所经历的深刻心理事件。根据现有文献，濒死体验通常包括四个主要特征。

首先，**灵魂出窍感**。人们报告感到自己漂浮在身体之外，俯视整个场景。一位参与者告诉我们：'我看到自己躺在医院床上，但我在天花板上。'

其次，**隧道与光明相遇**。许多人描述经历一条通道，通向明亮的光。他们说光是温暖的、欢迎的，一点都不害怕。

第三，**人生回顾**。人们看到整个人生在眼前闪过——不是混乱，而是有序的、有意义的审视。一位参与者说：'我看到了我每一个决定的影响——我对谁好，谁遭受了我的忽视。'

第四，**灵性觉知**。许多人报告感到与更大的宇宙力量相连，或遇到他们描述为'光'、'上帝'或'纯意识'的东西。

现在，这些体验有多普遍？全球研究表明，约4-15%的人口报告过濒死体验。在香港，我们几乎没有数据。这正是我们为什么选择在这里进行研究。"

**English:**
"A near-death experience, or NDE, is a profound psychological event occurring when someone faces death or life-threatening crisis. The literature identifies four core characteristics.

First, **out-of-body sensations**. People report floating outside their body, watching the scene from above. One participant told us: 'I saw myself on the hospital bed, but I was at the ceiling.'

Second, **tunnels and encounters with light**. Many describe a passage leading to bright light. They say the light is warm, welcoming—not frightening at all.

Third, **life review**. People see their entire life flash before their eyes—not chaotic, but an orderly, meaningful examination. One participant said: 'I saw the impact of every decision—who I helped, who I neglected.'

Fourth, **spiritual awareness**. Many report sensing connection to a greater universal force or encountering what they describe as 'the light,' 'God,' or 'pure consciousness.'

How common are these experiences? Global research suggests 4-15% of people report NDEs. In Hong Kong, we have almost no data. That's precisely why we chose to research here."

### 为什么要在香港研究？(Slide: Why Study in Hong Kong?) (2分钟 | 2 min)

**中文 Chinese:**
"香港是研究濒死体验的理想之地，原因有三个。

**第一，知识空白。** 国际濒死体验研究主要集中在欧美人群，特别是北美和欧洲。对亚洲——尤其是中文人群——的研究极其少见。我们想了解：濒死体验在香港是否以不同方式表现？文化是否塑造了这些体验的内容？

**第二，文化多样性。** 这非常重要。香港同时包含佛教（35.4%）、基督教（27.1%）、道教（20.8%）和世俗人群（16.7%）。这给了我们独特的科学机会——看到相同的心理现象如何在不同的文化和宗教框架中被解释和整合。

**第三，实际医疗意义。** 医疗专业人士常常将濒死体验视为幻觉或脑部缺氧的产物，这使经历过的人感到被否定和被病理化。但如果我们能证明这些体验带来真实的、可测量的心理转变，我们就能为医疗系统提供证据，改善对濒死体验者的护理和尊重。"

**English:**
"Hong Kong is ideal for studying NDEs for three reasons.

**First, knowledge gap.** International NDE research focuses overwhelmingly on Western populations—particularly North America and Europe. Research on Asian populations, especially Chinese speakers, is extremely rare. We wanted to know: do NDEs manifest differently in Hong Kong? Does culture shape the content of these experiences?

**Second, cultural diversity.** This is crucial. Hong Kong simultaneously includes Buddhist (35.4%), Christian (27.1%), Taoist (20.8%), and secular populations (16.7%). This gives us a unique scientific opportunity—to see how the same psychological phenomenon is interpreted and integrated within different cultural and religious frameworks.

**Third, practical medical significance.** Medical professionals often dismiss NDEs as hallucinations or mere brain hypoxia, leaving experiencers feeling invalidated and pathologized. But if we demonstrate that NDEs produce real, measurable psychological changes, we provide evidence to the medical system to improve care for and respect toward NDErs."

### 历史发展 (Slide: Historical Development of NDE Research) (3分钟 | 3 min)

**中文 Chinese:**
"感谢[A]。我是[名字]。现在我将为各位介绍濒死体验研究如何从个人观察发展为严格的科学学科。

**1892年——阿尔伯特·海姆。** 瑞士地质学家阿尔伯特·海姆与30名登山者进行了开创性工作。这些登山者在山难中濒临死亡，他们报告了快速的生命回顾和强烈的平静感。这是有记录的首次系统观察濒死现象。海姆的工作虽然在当时被忽视，但如今被认为是NDE研究的奠基石。

**1975年——雷蒙德·穆迪。** 快速前进一个世纪。心理学家雷蒙德·穆迪出版了《生命之后的生命》这本书。穆迪采访了150多位濒死体验者，仔细记录了他们的故事。基于这些访谈，穆迪提出了濒死体验的经典模型——包括灵魂出窍、光、隧道、人生回顾等特征。这本书改变了一切。它将NDE从奇异的个人故事转变为认真的学术研究对象。即便是今天，我们仍在使用穆迪的框架。

**1983年——布鲁斯·格雷森。** 然后是1983年，这是转折点。精神病学家布鲁斯·格雷森开发了格雷森NDE量表——第一个标准化的测量工具。这个量表允许研究人员以一致、可复现的方式测量NDE的深度和内容。从这一点开始，濒死体验研究从定性叙述变成了严格的定量科学。格雷森的工作使得进行真正的经验研究成为可能。"

**English:**
"Thank you [A]. I'm [Name]. I'll now walk you through how NDE research evolved from personal observation into rigorous science.

**1892—Albert Heim.** Swiss geologist Albert Heim conducted pioneering work with 30 mountaineers. These climbers faced death in mountain accidents and reported rapid life reviews and profound peace. This was the first systematic documented observation of near-death phenomena. Though overlooked at the time, Heim's work is now recognized as foundational to NDE research.

**1975—Raymond Moody.** Fast forward a century. Psychologist Raymond Moody published *Life After Life*. Moody interviewed over 150 NDErs, meticulously documenting their accounts. Based on these interviews, Moody proposed the classic model of NDEs—including out-of-body experiences, light, tunnels, life review. This book changed everything. It transformed NDEs from curious personal stories into serious academic research subjects. We still use Moody's framework today.

**1983—Bruce Greyson.** Then came 1983, a turning point. Psychiatrist Bruce Greyson developed the Greyson NDE Scale—the first standardized measurement tool. This scale allowed researchers to measure NDE depth and content in consistent, reproducible ways. From this point forward, NDE research shifted from qualitative narrative to rigorous quantitative science. Greyson's work made empirical research possible."

### 既有研究中的知识空白 (Slide: Existing Knowledge Gaps) (2分钟 | 2 min)

**中文 Chinese:**
"那么，既然我们有这么多国际研究，为什么还需要在香港研究？答案在于这些研究中存在的空白。

**地理偏差。** 大多数NDE研究集中在所谓的WEIRD人群——西方的、受教育的、工业化的、富有的、民主的人口。换句话说，主要是美国人和欧洲人。亚洲、非洲、拉丁美洲的非西方视角严重不足。

**方法限制。** 许多研究依赖于回顾性的自我报告——人们被问及多年前发生的事情。我们很少看到纵向研究，即追踪同一个人多年的变化。

**有限的结果焦点。** 既有研究主要关注死亡焦虑，但很少关注人格特质、价值观、灵性和其他深层心理变化。

**文化理解不足。** 也许最重要的是，文化框架与NDE内容之间的相互作用几乎没有被研究。例如，佛教框架和基督教框架如何塑造人们对NDE的解释？这个问题基本上未被探索。

这就是[演讲者C的名字]现在要为各位介绍的——我们的研究如何填补这些空白。"

**English:**
"So, given all this international research, why do we need to study NDEs in Hong Kong? The answer lies in gaps within this body of research.

**Geographic bias.** Most NDE research focuses on so-called WEIRD populations—Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic. Essentially, mostly American and European. Non-Western perspectives from Asia, Africa, and Latin America are severely underrepresented.

**Methodological limitations.** Many studies rely on retrospective self-reports—asking people about events years ago. We rarely see longitudinal studies tracking the same person over years.

**Limited outcome focus.** Existing research emphasizes death anxiety but seldom examines personality traits, values, spirituality, and other deep psychological changes.

**Insufficient cultural understanding.** Most importantly, how cultural frameworks interact with NDE content is barely studied. For instance, how do Buddhist versus Christian frameworks shape how people interpret NDEs? This question remains largely unexplored.

That's exactly what [Speaker C's name] will now introduce—how our research fills these gaps."

### 研究设计与样本 (Slide: Research Design & Sample) (1.5分钟 | 1 min 30 sec)

**中文 Chinese:**
"感谢[A和B]。我是[名字]。现在我将为各位详细介绍我们的混合方法研究。

我们进行了混合方法研究——结合定量和定性方法。

**定量部分：** 我们招募了30位香港濒死体验者。这些个体都经历过文件记录的危及生命的事件——心脏骤停、溺水、创伤或麻醉并发症。

**样本人口统计特征：**
- 53.3%女性，46.7%男性
- 平均年龄51.4岁（SD=13.8）
- 宗教分布反映香港多样性：36.7%佛教徒、26.7%基督教徒、20%道教徒、16.7%世俗人士
- 自濒死体验以来的平均时间：8.2年

**我们使用了三种标准化工具进行测量：**

1. **NEO五因素人格清单** — 测量五个主要人格维度
2. **施瓦茨价值观调查** — 评估什么对他们很重要
3. **科莱特-莱斯特死亡恐惧量表** — 量化他们对死亡的恐惧

我们将他们的分数与已发表的国际数据进行了比较。

**定性部分：** 我们进行了三次深入访谈，每次持续60-90分钟，与三位NDErs交谈——一位佛教徒、一位基督教徒、一位世俗人士——询问关于他们的体验、他们如何改变以及这对他们的信仰意味着什么的详细问题。"

**English:**
"Thank you [A and B]. I'm [Name]. Now I'll detail our mixed-methods research.

We conducted a mixed-methods study—combining quantitative and qualitative approaches.

**Quantitative part:** We recruited 30 Hong Kong NDErs. These individuals experienced documented life-threatening events—cardiac arrest, near-drowning, trauma, or anesthesia complications.

**Quantitative sample demographics:**
- 53.3% female, 46.7% male
- Mean age 51.4 years (SD=13.8)
- Religious distribution reflecting Hong Kong diversity: 36.7% Buddhist, 26.7% Christian, 20% Taoist, 16.7% secular
- Mean time since NDE: 8.2 years

**We measured using three standardized tools:**

1. **NEO Five-Factor Inventory** — measuring five core personality dimensions
2. **Schwartz Values Survey** — assessing what they consider important
3. **Collett-Lester Fear of Death Scale** — quantifying their fear of death

We compared their scores against published international data.

**Qualitative part:** We conducted three in-depth interviews, each 60-90 minutes, with three NDErs—one Buddhist, one Christian, one secular—asking detailed questions about their experience, how they changed, and what it meant for their beliefs."

### 研究方法论 (Slide: Research Methodology) (1.25分钟 | 1 min 15 sec)

**中文 Chinese:**
"我们的方法包括两个数据收集方法。

**定量方法：** 我们使用心理测量量表——这些是经过验证的、在心理学中使用了数十年的工具。参与者完成了关于人格、价值观和死亡焦虑的问卷。

**定性方法：** 我们进行了半结构化访谈，允许参与者详细讲述他们的经历，使用他们自己的话语和叙述。

**数据分析：** 对于定量数据，我们进行了描述性统计和t检验，将我们的发现与已发表的国际数据进行比较。对于定性数据，我们使用了六步主题分析方法：熟悉、编码、主题发展、精化、最终确定和报告。

这种混合方法方法让我们获得了数值数据的科学严谨性，同时也获得了定性叙述的人文深度。"

**English:**
"Our approach combined two data-collection methods.

**Quantitative method:** We used psychometric scales—validated instruments used in psychology for decades. Participants completed questionnaires on personality, values, and death anxiety.

**Qualitative method:** We conducted semi-structured interviews, allowing participants to elaborate on their experiences in their own words and narratives.

**Data analysis:** For quantitative data, we conducted descriptive statistics and t-tests, comparing our findings against published international data. For qualitative data, we used a six-step thematic analysis: familiarization, coding, theme development, refinement, finalization, and write-up.

This mixed-method approach gave us both the scientific rigor of numerical data and the human depth of qualitative narrative."

### 人格转变 (Slide: Personality Changes Post-NDE) (1.25分钟 | 1 min 15 sec)

**English:**

One participant told us: 'When I came back, I just wanted to help people. I used to care about my career and money. Now none of that matters.'

**Key finding:** NDEs catalyze profound, enduring personality transformation. These effects persist for decades—this isn't temporary."

### 灵性信念与死亡焦虑 (Slide: Religious & Spiritual Belief Shifts & Death Anxiety Reduction) (1分钟 | 1 min)

**中文 Chinese:**
"**发现三：信仰转变——从世俗到灵性**

在NDE之前，46.7%相信来世。之后——90%。这是一个巨大的转变。

佛教参与者——所有11位——将他们的体验与佛教宇宙论相连接。他们说："这正是《西藏死亡之书》描述的——光、存在、无时间性。这证实了佛教。"

即使是之前的世俗人士也改变了。一位说："我仍然不相信上帝，但我现在相信意识比我们想象的更基础。这给了我关于死亡的安宁。"

**发现四：死亡焦虑——大幅减少**

这是最显著的结果。

使用标准化的死亡焦虑量表，NDErs平均42.3，而对照组79.4。

用效应大小表示——这是一个*非常大*的效应，超过大多数心理治疗干预。

93.3%报告死亡恐惧几乎完全消除。

一位参与者说——我引用——'死亡曾经是我最大的恐惧。现在，在经历它之后，我完全平静。我知道死亡只是转变。没有什么可怕的。'"

**English:**
"**Finding Three: Belief shift—from secular to spiritual**

Before the NDE, 46.7% believed in an afterlife. After—90%. This is a massive shift.

Buddhist participants—all 11—connected their experience to Buddhist cosmology. They said: 'This is exactly what the Tibetan Book of the Dead describes—light, beings, timelessness. This confirms Buddhism.'

Even formerly secular people shifted. One said: 'I still don't believe in God, but I now believe consciousness is more fundamental than we thought. This gives me peace about death.'
